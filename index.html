About

WUHack 2014 is a hackathon meant for college students interested in technology hosted by Wash U's Upsilon Pi Epsilon chapter. UPE is the International Honor Society for the Computing and Information Disciplines. It will be hosted in St. Louis, Missouri, though students from anywhere are welcome!

Date: Sept 12th-14th
Size: 100-150 participants
Location: Washington University in St. Louis: Lopata Gallery

Email upe@cse.wustl.edu if you have any questions!

FAQ

What is WuHack?
WuHack is a weekend college student hackathon, the first of its type at Wash U. A hackathon is an event where technologists collaborate in teams to build/"hack" a project in a short span of time.
Who can attend?
Students of any type. As long as you are a student, you can participate for some prizes.
What types of hacks can teams make?
In short, pretty much anything! We're excited to see anything that you can come up with. HOWEVER, let's keep things safe, inoffensive, and legal. Besides that hack on!
What should I bring?
Your laptop, charger, and a hacker mindset. Bring a student ID for checking in as well. Hackers planning to stay on site overnight should bring toiletries and sleeping bags.
What are the team sizes?
Teams are made up of 2 to 5 students. It is not necessary to have a team before WuHack (in fact we encourage you to be open to meeting new people). We can help you put together a team!
Will food be provided?
Yup! We'll have food and drinks on hand for the entire event. Do not worry about going hungry. We'll have you covered.
What skills do I need to attend?
No skill but the ability to have a good attitude. Everyone is welcome regardless of skills. We will have mentors on board from a variety of companies to help develop your skills.
Can I start working on my hack before the official hackathon starts?
No. The point of a hackathon is to bring a new idea to life. Bringing an already started project violates the spirit of our hackathon (and our rules).
Why do you ask for my resume upon registration?
We pass your resumes to the companies that sponsor the event. Part of our mission is to connect ambitious and smart students with innovative and exciting companies.
